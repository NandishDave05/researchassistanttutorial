Legal and Business Considerations

Human Resources

Source: Enculescu Marian Vladut/Shutterstock.com

As a student participating on an internship or an employee of Humber working on a research project, you have the right to a healthy and safe workplace free from discrimination and harassment. All employees of Humber are mandated to complete training on the topics of human rights and diversity, and workplace health and safety. You will receive, or have already received, information about training sessions and links to relevant websites.

Please ensure you are familiar with your rights and responsibilities as a member of the college community. Your HR and diversity training will serve you well in illustrating these important concepts.

As an employee, you should also be aware of your employment rights and responsibilities. Government agency websites provide information on the following topics. Feel free to consult your college’s HR department if you want to clarify anything:

  • Workplace Safety and Insurance Board (WSIB) http://www.wsib.on.ca/
  • Employment insurance https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/ei.html
  • Wrongful dismissal and employment law http://www.canlii.org/en/commentary/wrongfuldismissal/
  • Ontario Human Rights Commission http://www.ohrc.on.ca/en
  • Humber’s policies and procedures http://www.humber.ca/policies/humber-policies-and-procedures
