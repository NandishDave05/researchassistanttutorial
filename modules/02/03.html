Legal and Business Considerations

General Business Etiquette And Standards Of Behaviour

Research projects involve at least a few – and sometimes a large number of – people coming from different types of organizations, so making sure everyone adheres to accepted business standards and etiquette will make working together more productive. Here are a few tips on conducting yourself as a student or research assistant:

Meet everyone and be courteous

Source: ASDF_MEDIA/Shutterstock.com

Your project lead or project manager will set up a team meeting so you can get to know everyone involved on the project. This is a professional setting and an opportunity for you to work with experts in your field of study. Enjoy yourself and bring the best you have to the team.

Be punctual and thorough

Source: Piotr Marcinski/Shutterstock.com

Start and finish your tasks on time. Let your teammates know if something unexpected comes up and you can’t make a meeting or complete a task. Fill out timesheets if required. Complete all tasks to the best of your ability.

Err on the side of caution and diplomacy

Source: Shutterstock.com

You can expect some sensitive information to be shared within the research team, in particular if the project involves a corporate or industry partner. Always be mindful of the partner organization’s interests and proprietary information (more on that in the next section!). Keep Humber’s reputation in mind at all times: you are representing a larger academic community and want to leave a good impression with everyone.

Ask questions, communicate

Source: ESB Professional/Shutterstock.com

If you don’t understand something, aren’t sure of the project tasks or the direction the project is taking, ask! Your project lead should guide you in the process and ensure you are learning from the experience, not wading in uncertainty.

Be curious and a true contributor

Source: ESB Professional/Shutterstock.com

This is the whole point of your research experience – do what you can to make the project a success, to move it forward, to contribute to solving the challenge at hand. You are here to learn and to offer your insights to the team.

Now, on to the core of legal matters in research!
