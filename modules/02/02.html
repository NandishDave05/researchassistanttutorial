Legal and Business Considerations

Introduction: Why Does The Legal And Business Stuff Matter?

When you work on an applied research project, you are likely to interact with a variety of people coming from different organizations. As a Humber student, the research project you are involved in can be a part of a course for academic credit or an independent project led by one of your professors for which you may be paid as a Research Assistant. Understanding the different roles of team members is important for making sure responsibilities are assigned properly and that everyone’s interests are acknowledged. This includes legal and business interests.

Why business and legal?

Research can be characterized as being either pure (basic), or applied.

Pure research is curiosity-driven (remember mixing substances in your chemistry class?) and uncovers the laws that govern the natural world.

In contract, applied research involves a practical and often immediate application for a user like a business or a non-profit organization.

Humber College has adopted the following definition of applied research – it acknowledges the different ways in which applied research opportunities arise:

Applied research is the systematic and original investigation of a problem, need or new entrepreneurial opportunity with real-world implications identified either by a third party (industry or community) or through analysis whose resolution will lead to a measurable outcome.

Since results of applied research most often have an immediate effect on a business or community organization, it is important that all parties involved fully understand the legal and business implications. For example, when you discover, through applied research, that there is a better way to build a machine that produces components of a multi-million dollar product, the corporate research partner is likely to want to protect the discovery through legal means, and make sure that the advantage is commercialized.

Humber College

Humber College

Humber is typically where the expert in the given research field is employed. This expert acts as a project lead or principal investigator (PI), the person leading the project and often managing its resources. This is typically someone very knowledgeable in the given research area – and one of your professors.

The Industry Or Community Partner

The Industry Or Community Partner

The industry or community partner may be a commercial entity, entrepreneur, government or not-for-profit organization faced with a challenge or problem they would like to solve with the help of Humber. Some research projects may involve more than one partner at the same time.

Research Assistant(s) (RAs)

Research Assistant(s) (RAs)

That’s you! As an RAs, you will assist the project lead in the process of conducting the research. You’ll learn in this tutorial about conducting a literature review, using different research methodology and report writing – all of these are typical tasks of the research assistant.

Together, the PI, partner(s), and RAs are the research team.

Sometimes, a project will have co-investigators in the same or different academic institutions or another organization. These can be other professors or specialists who help in leading the project. A project manager can also be assigned or hired onto a project to manage the research tasks and the resources (time, money, people, facilities and equipment) required for project completion. On a large and complex project, the principal investigator might not be the project manager. You’ll learn about project management in a later module.
