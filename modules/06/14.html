Recruiting Participants

Source: Monkey Business Images/Shutterstock.com

Now you know how many and what type of participants you need to recruit. How will you go about recruiting them?

If you are asked to assist a researcher in recruiting participants, he or she will probably have a plan in mind. If the recruitment involves students, you may be asked to go where the students (generally) are: their classrooms!

A generous teacher may offer up ten or fifteen minutes at the beginning or end of class for you to introduce the project and explain what informed consent means. You will also have to make it crystal clear that participation is entirely voluntary and that participants are free to withdraw at any time. It is especially important that in academic settings, students are well aware that their academic marks are not in any way contingent on their participation in the study.

You should also carry ID, dress professionally, smile, speak clearly, use the time efficiently, and thank the teacher on your way out the door. Do not leave a mess behind. Finally, go directly to a safe, secure place to store your data; most research projects must keep their data under lock and key at all times.
