In order to draw conclusions, a researcher needs data.

In order to gather data, a researcher needs data collection tools.

And in order to use data collection tools, a researcher needs participants!

In this module, you will learn about quantitative and qualitative data collection strategies, sampling, and how to recruit participants.

Earlier, you learned about the difference between quantitative and qualitative research. You may recall that both typically use distinct ways of collecting data. In this module, you are going to become familiar with some of the most common quantitative and qualitative data collecting methods. In the next module, you will learn about what to do with your data once it has been collected.

But first, let's start with quantitative data collection methods.

Quantitative Data Collection Methods

Recall that quantitative data is typically numerical: the data show things that can be counted and measured. This information can be gathered in a variety of ways. Here are a few:

  • Surveys
  • Observation
  • Document Review
