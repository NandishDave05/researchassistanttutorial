Data Analysis

Categorizing the Codes

Source: Shutterstock.com

The next step in qualitative data analysis is to take the coded statements out from their original data source and bunch them all together. The researcher then has a number of statements, typically on one page, referring to one topic.

The researcher may decide at this point that some codes are less interesting or less significant to the research at hand. These statements might get temporarily or permanently sidelined. Other codes might be so similar that they get merged under a single thematic category.

At this point, the researcher may begin to see some trends in the responses. She may discover, for example, that disparaging another team’s athleticism is often used immediately prior to competition to boost the rugby team’s confidence. She might also see that remarks related to the other team’s superior strength and strategy are used immediately after a loss to justify the pummelling.
