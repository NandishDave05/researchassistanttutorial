Overview

When you become a research assistant at Humber, at some point you may find yourself at a very exciting point in the research process: the point at which the majority of your data has been collected, and it is time to learn something from them. (Imagine it like you have all the ingredients for a delicious feast out on the kitchen counter before you, and all you have to do is put it together!). This next module will offer some guidance in the fine art and science of data analysis.

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize the differences between and calculate the mean, median, and mode
  • Name some methods for observing patterns and relationships in quantitative data
  • Define a confounding variable and identify the difficulties in showing causation
  • Name the common data analysis approaches in qualitative research
  • Code and categorize qualitative data

Main Topics

  • Quantitative Data Analysis
  • Qualitative Data Analysis

Checkpoint Review

Before completing Module 7, students must have completed Module 1-5 and confidently answered the following review questions in their journals:

  1. What are the first steps in the research process?
  2. What are some characteristics of good research?
  3. What are some key differences between qualitative and quantitative research approaches?
  4. What are some principles guiding ethical research practices at Humber (and elsewhere in Canada), and how are these principles managed?
  5. What are the characteristics of a “project”?
  6. How does a researcher create a work plan?
  7. What are some key communication and conflict resolution skills that will be required of research assistants at Humber?
  8. What is the value in completing a lit review, and what are the steps?
  9. What are the most common quantitative and qualitative data collection methods?
  10. How are participants recruited for a research project?
