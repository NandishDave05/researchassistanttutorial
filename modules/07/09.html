Coding the Data

Once data has been compiled and read through at least two or three times, many qualitative researchers like to do what's called coding the data.

Coding the data is a process that makes the next step of analysis a little easier. Researchers use numbers, letters, or even short words to indicate the topic being addressed in the data. Before developing his codes, a researcher must decide what units will be coded.

Source: Baggesen, Rikke Haller. (Photographer). (2013). Coded transcripts from workshop II + III. [Digital image], Retrieved from

For example, assume our researcher wants to understand the way in which the varsity men's rugby team positions itself in relation to other college teams against which they will be competing. After reading and re-reading through the stack of data, the researcher begins to see some "chunks" of data that can have codes attached to them:

  • The other team is athletically stronger and will beat us
  • The other team is athletically stronger, requiring our full efforts
  • The other team is athletically weaker and we will beat them easily
  • The other team is athletically weaker. We can’t let that negatively affect our performance.

The researcher then attributes a code to each kind of statement.

Statements and their Codes
ASB The other team is athletically stronger and will beat us
ASE The other team is athletically stronger, requiring our full efforts
AWB The other team is athletically weaker and we will beat them easily
AWNP The other team is athletically weaker. We can’t let that negatively affect our performance

The next step is to work through the data again and code the statements that are alike with ASB, ASE, AWB, AWNP. It would not be unusual for a researcher to have twenty or thirty codes in this initial stage of analysis.
