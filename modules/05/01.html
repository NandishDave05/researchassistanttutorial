Overview

In Module 1, you were introduced to the important steps in the research process. Let's review them again here.

  1. Develop a research question
  2. Create a hypothesis or research objective
  3. Select the best method based on the question being asked
  4. Conduct a literature review
  5. Collect data
  6. Analyse data
  7. Finally, present your findings

In this Module, you will learn the specifics of the literature review: one of the key steps listed above.

First, let's review the whats: what is a literature review (often referred to as lit review)? Then, you will learn the whys: three reasons that researchers conduct literature reviews. Finally, you will learn the hows: how do you set about conducting a literature review?

We will end this module with some thought as to how you might assist a supervising researcher with his or her literature review.

Learning Objectives

At the completion of this Module, students will be able to:

  • Describe the reasons for literature reviews
  • Name the major components of literature reviews
  • Outline the steps required in writing a literature review

Main Topics

  • What is a literature review?
  • Why do we write literature reviews?
  • How do we write literature reviews?
  • How might you assist a supervising researcher to write a literature review?

Checkpoint Review

Before completing Module 5, students must have completed Module 1-4 and confidently answered the following review questions in their journals:

  1. What are the first steps in the research process?
  2. What are some characteristics of good research?
  3. What are some key differences between qualitative and quantitative research approaches?
  4. What are some principles guiding ethical research practices at Humber (and elsewhere in Canada), and how are these principles managed?
  5. What are the characteristics of a project?
  6. How does a researcher create a work plan?
  7. What are some key communication and conflict resolution skills that will be required of research assistants at Humber?
