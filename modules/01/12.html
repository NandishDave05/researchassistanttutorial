Differences Between the Two Approaches

As you may have already noticed, qualitative and quantitative research processes are very different. One way to imagine the difference is to consider that:

  • quantitative research tends to start with a theory and then test it,
  • while qualitative research begins by gathering data and then attempts to form a theory from that data.

gpointstudio/Shutterstock.com

One researcher, Deborah K. Van Den Hoonaard, asks us to imagine vacation planning as an analogy for research methods (2012). If you were planning a trip qualitatively, you might gather multiple guidebooks, book a flight, and then see where each day takes you, consulting the guide books as you go if necessary. You might be pleasantly surprised by an unexpectedly delightful discovery, but you might equally miss a must-see stop. At the end of the trip, you might reflect back on the best moments and take note of them for future friends and family who might enjoy a similar holiday. Conversely, if you were planning a trip quantitatively, you might sign up for a package tour: much of the planning is done up front, and you can be sure you're going to hit the hot spots. You're not likely to encounter many surprises, but you're pretty much locked into whatever the tour has in store for you.
