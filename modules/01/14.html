Summary & Next Steps

Now, you should be able identify the observation that underpins a research question and assess a research question for clarity and focus. You can distinguish between operational and non-operational variables and describe the advantages of performing a thorough literature review. You should be able to list the characteristics of good research. You've learned how to distinguish between qualitative and quantitative research approaches and explain the reasons a researcher might select one method over the other. Finally, you should be able to identify the advantages of mixed-method approaches.

Understanding the key steps in the research process and the justification for those steps is essential. It will enable you to conduct research in a disciplined and reliable fashion. The next concept you will learn in this course, research ethics, is equally important. The management and enforcement of strict research ethics standards ensures that both researchers and participants are sufficiently protected from harm during research. A project's potential for harm must be carefully scrutinized and managed at all steps of the research project. Many of the major problems, however, can be effectively eliminated with careful planning at the early stages.
