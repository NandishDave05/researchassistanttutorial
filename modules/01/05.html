Hypothesis or Research Objective

HYPOTHESIS: an educated prediction about the presumed findings of the project.

After a research question has been established, the hypothesis forms the starting point for research. Simply put it is an attempt to answer the research question at the outset of the research. In other words, a hypothesis is an educated prediction about the presumed findings of the project.

 

Source: Stuart Jenner/Shutterstock.com

In many research projects, the hypothesis also clarifies the variables examined in the research. Variables are things that produce or undergo change that can be measured.

Consider the second sample research question about student frustration. It’s impossible to measure frustration with a ruler or a thermometer. So, researchers must devise some other method of measuring frustration. It may be possible to do this with some sort of brain scan that registers change. Or, researchers could devise a survey that asks participating students to indicate their levels of frustration (alternatively, researchers can “borrow” survey tools that are already developed – much like borrowing a thermometer or ruler). That way, the variable of frustration is operationalized: meaning, made “workable” in a research project.

Those variables are then presented in the hypothesis in terms of their predicted relationship with one another. For example, a hypothesis might be that when academic progress is regularly made available on the course website, survey results will show that students’ frustration upon seeing a change in their end-of-term mark will be lessened. Variable A (the regular posting of academic progress) will correlate with a decrease in Variable B (the reported feelings of frustration as measured by a survey).

Do the researchers know that this is what their data will show? No. A hypothesis is only an educated prediction. The findings of the research may support or disprove this prediction.

Acute Effects of Exercise on the Cortical Silent Period in Prostate Cancer Patients

Hypothesis: We hypothesize that those who undergo 60 minutes of exercise will show significant increases (improvement) in their cortical silent period compared to the control group. We also hypothesize that changes in cortical silent period will be positively associated with changes in self-reported mood.

In Place and In Practice: An Evaluation of School-Based Strategies that Support Adolescents with an Ill Parent

Hypothesis: It is anticipated that teachers will relay a variety of initiatives (both school-broad and classroom-specific) that work to support student mental health in college settings. It is anticipated that support specifically for adolescents with an ill parent will be underrepresented; however, it is expected that discussions will be held on how this population can be helped through existing, generic support systems. Furthermore, it is anticipated that teachers will identify and learn where initiatives can be strengthened to better support student mental health.

Examining Student Knowledge Enhancement Following Simulation Experience

Hypothesis: It is anticipated that the subjects in the test group will demonstrate improved scores on the post-test when compared with the subjects in the control group.

Some kinds of research do not attempt to show relationships between two variables. In this case, the hypothesis is sometimes replaced with a research objective. The research objective describes what social event or context will be the focus of the investigation. For example, a research objective might be “to identify some trends in decision making pertaining to what day of the week to visit the Humber Room for lunch.”

The Development of a Video Game to Increase Student Engagement

Objective: We will design and develop a video game focused on increasing student engagement. By the term “engagement” we refer to students that keep up with course material, are motivated to overcome challenges, and participate in activities that are conducive to learning.

 

Time, Movement Patterns, and Safety Incidents in the FX Child Care Kindergarten Playground

Objective:It is the objective of this study to gather data on safety incidents, as well as the location of child play and movement patterns.

The Effects of Hemp Protein Supplementation in Male and Female Endurance Athletes following Chronic Intra-session Concurrent HIIT and Strength Training

Objective: It is the purpose of this study to identify whether a protein supplement (plant-based or animal-based) following intra-session bouts of concurrent training will maximize performance among physically active males and females.
