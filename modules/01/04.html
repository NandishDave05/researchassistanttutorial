Begin with an Observation

OBSERVATION: Most credible research begins with an observation about the world.

It might be something like,

  • Example B

I observe that that the line-ups at Donut Shop A comprise mostly young people, while the line-ups at Donut Shop B comprise people of all ages.

I observe that students feel surprised and frustrated when their midterm marks and their end-of-term marks are dramatically different.

I observe that more people visit the Humber Room restaurant on Fridays more than any other day of the week.

All of these and many more are examples of observations and observations typically form the seed of a research project.

Source: Aaron Amat/Shutterstock.com

TIP : Make sure you understand the observation that sparked the next research project you are engaged with. If it's not clear to you, just ask!

 

The Research Question

The next step in the research process involves devising a research question from the observation. Research questions must be clear and focused.

Some examples might be,

Click the tabs to read the examples.
  • Example A
  • Example B
  • Example C

What techniques have been employed at Donut Shop A to attract customers of all ages?

Will students’ surprise and frustration decrease if their academic progress in the course is made visible to them on Blackboard?

What are some reported reasons that people visit the Humber Room on Fridays?

The research question is usually explicitly stated in a research project. A good research question focuses the research and can even give hints about the methods the researchers will use.
