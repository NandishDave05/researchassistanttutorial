Research Posters & Powerpoint Presentations

Many researchers disseminate their findings at conferences. Typically, these are events where many people with shared research interests congregate to share knowledge. Often, contributors are asked to display their work in poster or presentation format. Both require researchers to summarize their process and present their findings, but their formats are slightly different.

Research Posters

Research posters are meant to engage people and promote discussion. They should contain the most important elements of the research with a limited number of words (some people suggest no more than 800). They should be visually interesting and use photos, tables, charts, and illustrations if possible. Often, people spend only two or three minutes viewing a poster so the important information must be concise. Sometimes people circulate is small groups, so the text must be printed large enough for people to see from at least a metre away.

Powerpoint Presentations

Source: YURALAITS ALBERT/Shutterstock.com

There is some debate as to the best way to make use of PowerPoint technology. Some would say the best presenters use the slides simply for images, charts, and tables. Other say a minimal use of words and phrases to emphasize certain key points is acceptable. But most would agree that a slide cluttered with tiny, long paragraphs of words is completely useless (let alone unattractive).
