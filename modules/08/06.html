Tips for Writing Research Reports

Source: Calkins/Shutterstock.com

In your role as a research assistant, you may be asked to submit short or long pieces of writing that may be included in the final report. Here are some guidelines, recommended by Martyn Denscombe (2010), to keep in mind as you prepare to submit your writing to your supervisor:

Select each section to reveal the information.

1. Use the third person
1. Use the third person

Conventional research reports require writers to leave out pronouns like I, we, or you. Occasionally, researchers may use these pronouns to “position themselves” in qualitative research, where the subjective perspective and/or biases of the researcher are very important to establish. Even in these cases, however, some researchers reserve the use of first person pronouns for only some sections of the report.

2. Use the past tense

2. Use the past tense

Given that the majority of your report will include a recollection of past events, this guideline tends to be fairly easy to follow.

3. Be careful with your grammar and spelling

3. Be careful with your grammar and spelling

Nothing detracts from credibility more than careless writing. Be sure to edit and re-edit your work before you submit it.

4. Regulatory Requirements

4. Develop logical transitions that take the reader from one section to the next

Most people reading the final report will be accustomed to the basics of the research process. They will likely be expecting to see familiar patterns in the way the findings are reported. Using headings and sub-headings can make the sections even more clearly differentiated.

5. Tables, figures, and charts should be consistent

5. Tables, figures, and charts should be consistent

This is particularly true of quantitative research. For example, if the x-axis on one chart shows numbers 1-100, another chart measuring a similar value should not show only numbers 10-20. This angle of the line chart might be deceptively steeper in the second chart and could accidentally mislead the reader.
