Overview

In previous modules you’ve learned how to plan a research project, collect data, and analyze that data to answer a research question. Once you’ve analyzed data and answered your research question, your research project is complete, right? Actually, a very important step in the research process is still left: disseminating your research process and results! In this next module, you will learn about some important features of research reports and presentations.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the most important components of research reports
  • List and describe the characteristics of credible research: validity, reliability and generalizability, and objectivity
  • Distinguish between summarizing and interpreting data
  • Identify some major differences in the reporting of qualitative and quantitative research
  • Assist in the preparation of a research report or presentation for a variety of audiences

Main Topics

  • Most important components of research reports
  • Characteristics of credible research reports
  • Summarizing and Interpreting the Data
  • Differences between qualitative and quantitative research reports
  • Tips for Writing Research Reports
  • Which audiences call for which type of presentation?

Checkpoint Review

Before completing Module 8, students must have completed Module 1-5 and confidently answered the following review questions in their journals:

  1. What are the first steps in the research process?
  2. What are some characteristics of good research?
  3. What are some key differences between qualitative and quantitative research approaches?
  4. What are some principles guiding ethical research practices at Humber (and elsewhere in Canada), and how are these principles managed?
  5. What are the characteristics of a “project”?
  6. How does a researcher create a work plan?
  7. What are some key communication and conflict resolution skills that will be required of research assistants at Humber?
  8. What is the value in completing a lit review, and what are the steps?
  9. What are the most common quantitative and qualitative data collection methods?
  10. How are participants recruited for a research project?
  11. What are key skills used in the analysis of quantitative data?
  12. What are key skills used in the analysis of qualitative data?
