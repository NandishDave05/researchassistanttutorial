There are three major funding bodies for research undertaken in Canada:

  • Canadian Institutes of Health Research,
  • Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada,
  • Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada.

Itâ€™s likely that any research project with which you are involved will have, at some point, received direct or indirect funding from one or more of these agencies.

In 1998, these Agencies banded together with the objective of promoting ethical standards for research involving human participants. These standards are influenced by leading international norms, norms which have been in development for the last 70 years.

Simply put, the Agencies will not fund research that does not meet their research standards. This means the Agencies must somehow monitor researchers' commitment to ethical research. They do this by ensuring that each research institute creates a Research Ethics Board (REB).

The Humber Research Ethics Board is made up of ten faculty members and administrators and represents the interests of the Agencies by evaluating project applications. Any project at Humber using human participants must be granted approval by the Humber Research Ethics Board.
